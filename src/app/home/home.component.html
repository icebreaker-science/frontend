About Icebreaker

With our new platform Icebreaker.science, we aim to showcase your expertise on methods and devices, enabling you to find cooperation partners in chemistry and related disciplines. This holds the potential to generate new collaborative research ideas.

Almost every chemical problem requires exploration by qualitative or/and quantitative analysis. Scientists from various disciplines use many analytical methods daily; some are directly available in their labs or central analytics departments. This infrastructure covers mainly standard instrumentation. Some research questions, however, call for specialized devices, new methods, and external expertise. In these cases, it is important to find possible cooperation partners within the same institution or beyond. According to our survey (link to Community), over 70% of such collaborations come about through personal interactions (conferences, established contacts of professors/supervisors, lectures). However, the COVID-19 pandemic shows that digitization can and must establish alternatives here as well.

In order to get a comprehensive picture of the situation, we created a survey that included questions about literature research and questions about general challenges in everyday research and during the COVID-19 pandemic. A total of 127 participants from different universities took part in the survey.

Device sharing and networking during COVID-19

Due to difficult research conditions in the course of contact restrictions, inefficient laboratory occupancy, and lack of equipment availability, the participants observed a slowing down of exchange and networking. In particular, the young scientists criticized the limited informal exchange, the great uncertainty, and the limited ability to plan, which often led to time delays. Finding the right contact person and the appropriate method also proved to be particularly difficult. We suspect that researchers who are at the beginning of their scientific careers and do not yet have a large network and established contacts are particularly disadvantaged here.

Our new approach for knowledge discovery

Especially for students and doctoral candidates getting started with their research, having a roadmap would help them find their way into a new research topic. For this, we aim to create a new tool to visualize current research as a network, which can be explored. Through the exploration, new keywords and connections between fields can be discovered. Ideally, papers can be found directly through this process. Given our currently small database, this is not yet possible, but the network can be used in combination with conventional search platforms.

Community

According to our survey, over 70% of collaborations result from personal interaction in lectures, conferences, or with the help of professors and supervisors. However, the COVID-19 pandemic has severely limited in-person interaction. Therefore, we see a chance to support young scientists in building a platform, which holds all relevant information to find future collaborators.

Icebreaker.science can help by providing:

  • An overview of entire community
  • A possibility to promote their expertise
  • A way to meet and contact other experts and possible collaboration partners

Given our currently small database, this is not yet possible, but the network can be used in combination with conventional search platforms.

So far it is possible to:

  • Chose one of the provided fields
  • Search or add interesting keywords to reduce graph and show relevant connections
  • Click on edges to see linked papers
